Original Writing

Voices we gave
space to breathe.

Excerpts from original screenplays, commissioned pieces, and personal projects developed within Dorsey Films. Each began as a feeling that needed a body — a specific voice in a specific room.

01 — Before the Shift

The Toll Booth on Route 9

"My father worked this same stretch for twenty-two years. Never complained, never called in sick. I used to think that made him strong. Now I'm not so sure it wasn't just fear — the quiet kind that doesn't have a name yet. The kind that looks like responsibility from far enough away."

— Documentary Feature, 2022. Character: Frank Malone, 58.
02 — After the Diagnosis

What the Body Remembers

"People keep telling me to fight. Like it's a war I chose. Like there's a version of this where I win by wanting it badly enough. But some things aren't about winning. Some things are just about staying in the room as long as you can — paying attention, not pretending the light isn't beautiful just because it's leaving."

— Short Film, 2023. Character: Ruth Adeleke, 67.
03 — The Morning After

Fourteen Seconds of Silence

"We'd been together so long I'd forgotten how to eat a meal alone without feeling watched. Not by her. By the person I used to be before I learned to share everything — the salt, the remote, the window seat, the dark."

— Narrative Short, 2021. Character: David Asher, 44.
04 — The Return

Childhood Home, For Sale

"The rooms are smaller than I remembered. The ceilings lower. I keep reaching for light switches that aren't there anymore. I don't know what I came looking for. I think I came to let it go. But grief doesn't work like a key — you don't just hand it over and get your hands back."

— Documentary Essay, 2024. Voice: Carla Hendricks, 51.
05 — Overture

The First Take

"Every film starts with someone willing to be uncomfortable — the director who doesn't know yet if the idea is good, the actor standing in a parking lot at 5am pretending it's 1987, the editor choosing which truth to keep. We do this because something in us believes that a story, told honestly, can reach across the distance between two strangers and make them feel — for one exact moment — less alone. That's the job. That's always been the job."

— Brand Film, Dorsey Films Identity, 2024.

The Team

The people
behind the frame.

Small by design. Every person here has worked long enough to know what they care about — and what to turn down when it doesn't fit.

Director / Founder

Peirre Dorsey

Peirre founded Dorsey Films in 2016 after seven years as a cinematographer for hire. He directs all feature and documentary work, believing that the most important shot is always the one the script didn't write. Born in Pittsburgh, trained in Prague.

Director of Photography

Nadia Vasquez

Nadia has shot every Dorsey Films project since 2018. Trained at ESCAC Barcelona, she spent four years operating on narrative features across Europe before returning stateside. She shoots on film whenever the budget allows — and doesn't apologize for either choice.

Editor / Colorist

James Okoro

James joined in 2019 after cutting four independent features. He works primarily in DaVinci Resolve and believes the edit is the second screenplay. His color work has been recognized by the International Cinematographers Guild. He teaches documentary editing at Carnegie Mellon.

Sound Designer / Composer

Elin Strand

Originally from Gothenburg, Elin studied electroacoustic composition before moving into film sound. She has designed audio for fourteen shorts and three features. She believes the right silence is worth more than any music — and handles all original score and final mix for Dorsey Films.

Our Story

Built on the belief
that specificity
is everything.

Dorsey Films was founded in a two-room studio in Pittsburgh's Strip District with one camera, a secondhand light kit, and a conviction that the stories closest to the truth are the ones that feel like nothing you've seen before.

Eight years later, the instinct is unchanged: find the frame nobody else would choose, stay in it long enough, and trust what happens. We've made films for independent artists, global nonprofits, major brands, and public television. The work varies. The standard doesn't.

We are a small studio by deliberate choice. We turn down work we're wrong for. We take on projects that scare us a little. We believe a ten-minute film that actually reaches someone is worth more than an hour of technically accomplished nothing.
